Where

At School

StratoSim tools fit nicely into undergraduate and high school engineering and physics classes. Students can easily create and simulate circuits online, embed them on a class blog, or export them for reports. Interested in trying it out in your class? Talk to us about setting up our tools to make your life easy by sending you submission reports, integrating your toolbox, and much more!

At Work

We're actively working on getting our tool ready for professionals. Our simulator is user friendly, collaborative and cloud based for the future. Your data is always stored securely and backed up to multiple servers. You can even use PSPICE or HSPICE or your own models instead of the default Ngspice by contacting us.

At Home

Hobbyists can quickly document and test their designs during while building the next big thing. Designs are versioned and backed up, so it's easy to revert if needed. By sharing designs using a unique URL and embed widget, others can give feedback and build upon existing designs. Try it for your next project!

Features

Always Accessible

Your designs will be accessible from any modern web browser, meaning you could build it on your desktop in your room and instantly have a friend view it in lab. There's also no need to worry about what systems your teammates are using since StratoSim will work with most major browsers.

Safe and Secure

Because designs are safely stored and backed up in the cloud, you can be sure that they'll be there even if you drop your laptop. Circuits are saved periodically so you can always look at revision history. Collaborators are specified by an access control list set by you.

Intuitive Interface

We take great care to ensure that schematic drawing feels natural. Instead of mode toggling, wires begin to route as soon as you click on a pin and stop routing when you click on another pin. You can always move devices and wires by dragging them. Wires intelligently follow devices when they move or rotate to making it easy to edit designs.

SPICE Simulation

SPICE simulation is done with Ngspice, a widely used open source simulator. After drawing your circuit, you can quickly configure simulation settings, simulate your design and view results, without ever leaving your browser. You can also export your data or download SPICE for analysis with other tools. Rather use PSPICE or HSPICE or your own models? Talk to us about integrating your organization's simulation tools.

Simple Sharing

Working in a team is simplified with StratoSim Circuit Simulator. All your team members can simultaneously view circuits, and a full revision history lets everyone see how changes are made. Collaboration is also great for students working together on an assignment, or for instructors to quickly post simulations online.

Pretty Publishing

Designs and charts can be easily exported to a variety of formats, including PDF, PNG, SVG, and EPS. This makes it easy to print your designs, include them in reports and publications and send them to other people. Data can also be exported to CSV to be processed with more advanced graphing tools, and generated SPICE can be downloaded for doing more detailed simulations locally.

Easy Embedding

Circuits and charts can be embedded in blogs and websites using the customizable StratoSim widget. This allows your viewers to easily download circuits and charts. They can also open them with StratoSim Circuit Simulator, and run their own modified simulations. Circuit and chart images may also be directly embedded in blogs, websites and emails.

Coming Soon

We're working hard on a bunch of improvements including tablet support, real time collaboration, more types of simulation and better device libraries. We're also exploring ways to allow you to easily import from and export to other schematic editors and simulation tools. We always welcome your suggestions.

Technology

HTML5

Our product is based on HTML5 and JavaScript. We always use current web standards to ensure a fluid experience from any modern web browser.

Ngspice

Simulations are performed by running Ngspice on our servers. Ngspice is a mixed mode simulator based on Spice3f5, XSpice and Cider.
