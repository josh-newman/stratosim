Getting Started

Thanks for trying StratoSim Circuit Simulator! The best way to get started with StratoSim is to dive in and build a simple circuit. This tutorial will walk you through building an RC circuit and performing transient analysis with StratoSim.

 
Next >>

Placing Devices

We'll start by placing a DC voltage source. Click on the voltage source icon on the device panel. We want to place the default 1V DC source.

Next >>

Placing Devices

To place the device, click the device in the device panel and then click on the drawing area. You can always press escape to get out of placing mode (or any mode).

Rotating and Moving Devices

<< Prev

Rotating and Moving Devices

Editing Parameters

Now let's make the capacitor 40uF. Click on the label to bring up the parameter editor. Click on the "Value" text box and enter 40uF.

<< Prev
Next >>

Editing Parameters

The box turns green for valid values and red for invalid ones. Values can be expressed in scientific or engineering notation and are followed by the unit. For example, 40uF, 4e-5F and 0.00004F are all valid values.

<< Prev
Next >>

Editing Parameters

The standard spice suffixes (m, u, n, p, k, meg, g, t) are all supported for engineering notation. You can close the editor by pressing enter, or clicking a blank part of the circuit.

Next >>

Placing Wires

Time to connect the devices! Click on the top pin of the voltage source (the orange dot). As you move your mouse, a wire segment will follow. Click on the pin on the left side of the resistor to place the segment.

<< Prev
Next >>

Placing Wires

Place all the remaining wires. Clicking on a blank part of the circuit toggles between vertical and horizontal. Pins change from orange to black to indicate they've been connected. You can always cancel wire mode by pressing escape.

<< Prev
Next >>

Connect on Place

Devices will automatically connect to wires if their pin is on top of a wire when placed or dragged. Let's try it. Click on the ground icon in the device panel (under the sources tab).

<< Prev
Next >>

Connect on Place

Line up the ground's shadow so that the pin is on top of the bottom wire. When you click to place, the pin is automatically connected to the wire.

<< Prev
Next >>

Connect on Place

Printing

Time to download our amazing RC circuit! Click on the print icon on the top right to download the circuit as a pdf. The circuit can now be printed or emailed as a pdf.

<< Prev
Next >>

Printing

Depending on your brow-fluidser, clicking print may prompt you to download, or display it in a new window. You can also choose png, svg or eps to download the file in these formats.

Simulating

Now for the real fun! Click the lightning bolt at the top right to perform transient analysis on the circuit.

<< Prev
Next >>

Simulating

The graphs displayed correspond to the probes you've placed in the circuit. Probes with the same "group" parameter are plotted on the same plot. You can also change the color parameter to red, orange, yellow, green, blue and purple.

<< Prev
Next >>

Simulating

The circuit is not editable during simulation mode, but parameters can be viewed and the circuit can be zoomed and panned just as in edit mode. To go back into editing, click the lightning bolt toggle button.

Saving
